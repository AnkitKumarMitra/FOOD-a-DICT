Pindi Chole

i guess by now, you must be wondering what is the difference between the regular chole masala and pindi chole masala. well, certainly there are some notable differences which make pindi chana unique and flavoured. the former recipe is made with a regular tomato and onion curry base with not so much spices.

Nutrition Facts
Amount per serving
Calories 324
Total Fat 10g
Total Carbohydrate 25g
Protein 34g

Ingredients

for pressure cooking:


▢1 cup chana / chickpea
▢2 tea bags
▢1 inch cinnamon / dalchini
▢1 bay leaf / tej patta
▢3 pods cardamom / elachi
▢4 cloves / lavang
▢¼ tsp baking soda
▢½ tsp salt
▢3 cup water

for chana masala spice powder:
▢½ tsp cumin / jeera
▢1 tsp coriander seeds
▢¼ tsp shahi jeera
▢¼ tsp pepper
▢¼ tsp fennel / saunf
▢1 tsp kasuri methi / dry fenugreek leaves
▢3 cloves
▢2 pods cardamom / elachi
▢3 dried red chilli
▢¼ tsp turmeric
▢¼ tsp aamchur / dry mango powder
▢¼ tsp pomegranate powder
▢pinch hing / asafoetida

for curry:
▢3 tsp oil
▢1 bay leaf / tej patta
▢1 onion (finely chopped)
▢1 tsp ginger garlic paste
▢1 chilli (slit)
▢2 tomato (finely chopped)
▢½ tsp salt
▢2 tbsp coriander (finely chopped)

for tempering:
▢1 tbsp ghee / clarified butter
▢1 chilli (slit)
▢1 inch ginger (julienne)
▢¼ tsp kashmiri red chilli powder

Method


Prepare the chicken:
firstly, in a large kadai heat 3 tsp oil and saute 1 bay leaf.
further add 1 onion followed by 1 tsp ginger garlic paste and 1 chilli.
saute well until the onions turn golden brown.
now add prepared spice powder.

saute on low flame until the spice powder turn aromatic.
further add 2 tomato and saute well.
cook until the tomatoes turn mushy and oil is separated.
now add cooked chana and ½ tsp salt.
furthermore, cover and simmer for 15 minutes.
also add 2 tbsp coriander and mix well.
finally, enjoy pindi chole with roti or bhatura.
